Korean Names and Meanings

In South Korea, names hold significant meanings both personally and concerning family history. Common themes found in Korean names include favored characteristics such as wisdom, strength, kindness, and references to nature. Korean names are typically associated, largely thanks to South Korea’s significant influence through its music and TV shows, with romance, modernity, and charm.

The system of writing Korean names adds an extra layer of uniqueness, as they can have much more detailed meanings than other names. Korean is written in Hangul, the Korean alphabet, created in 1445 by King Sejong the Great. Previously, Chinese scripts were used in Korea. Hanja is this older Chinese script used alongside Hangul in names, with each Hanja character having its unique meaning. Many Hanja used in Korean names represent references to nature and ideal character traits and can give information on someone’s genealogy and family history. In this guide, you will find a curated list of the most popular Korean names and have organized lists of names according to the impressions they make. Please note these names may have multiple Hanja or Hangul variations, and their meanings can slightly differ based on the chosen characters.
